Speed control of a DC motor using PID, Pole Placement and LQR controllers:



DC motor parts

DC motor plays a significant role in modern industry. There are several types of applications where the load on the DC motor varies over a speed range. These applications may demand high-speed control accuracy and good dynamic responses.



Simulink representation

In this project, The mathematical model of the system was obtained. MATLAB Simulink was used to study the dynamic behavior of the system using the model obtained. The performance of the obtained models is compared with the experimental results.




Speed response of a closed loop system due to step input

Speed response of the close loop system due to step input with disturbance



Control signal of the close loop system due to step input



Control signal of the close loop system due to step input with disturbance

Based on the results obtained, it can be concluded that pole placement where the dominant poles are complex poles and the other poles are real poles and are located far away from the imaginary axis contributed to a better settling of the system with zero overshoot but at the expense of very large energy consumption. LQR controller contributed to a delayed settling of the system with zero overshoot but with small energy consumption. PID controller contributed to a fast settling of the system more than LQR and overshoot which is more than that of LQR and Pole placement
